Scrypick data blog

Notes from the draft data.

Short, honest reads on drafting League of Legends: synergy and matchup edges measured from a large ranked corpus, with the methodology in the open. No tier-list hand-waving.

  • Method · Item value

    Why win rate lies about items

    An item's raw win rate mostly measures the situation it gets bought in, not the item. Defensive items look weak because you buy them behind; snowball items look strong because you buy them ahead. Why we won't rank items by win rate, and the debiased measure, Win Probability Added, we're building instead.
  • Data · Itemization

    The builds the ladder has solved, and the ones it hasn't

    Some champions have one forced core item bought in 96% of games; others are a coin-flip where the most popular build is still a minority. A map of build consensus across 115,000 ranked games, measured as popularity, with an honest line on why that isn't the same as strength.
  • Data · Synergy & matchups

    The strongest champion pairings in the current meta

    Which duos actually lift each other, which drag each other down, and the hardest lane counters, measured from a verified-clean ranked corpus with a recency-weighted, shrinkage-corrected estimator. Includes the pairs the meta over-rates, and the bug we fixed to trust our own numbers.
